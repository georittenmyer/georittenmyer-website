After taking a few years working on my personal life, I am back and ready to share! I drove up to Stevens Pass, WA on a cold and clear morning to get some fresh air, fresh tracks and some fresh photographs. The temperature was a chilly 9º at 8:30am, and one thing I learned from living on the east coast, the colder the day, the clearer the sky. Why? The colder air holds less water which in return gives less light refraction.
